# A Fresh Start

## Starting A Blog
So this whole project of writing a blog is new to me.
I mean, I've tried and failed a million times before. But this time, I hope, will be different.
See, it started with the realization that, as someone who is... let's say attentionally challenged, the spreading thin of my attention in all the various directions it goes without any real concerted effort in one or two areas leaves me making very little real progress. I have a lot of dreams, hopes, and interests. I've even got some talent and curiousity to back some of those things up. But without focus, I'll make a little progress here and there, but nothing substantial will come of it. And given how quickly the trivial comes to me, I grow bored with the shallow progress steps. 

Starting this blog is my way of, in a certain sense, keeping myself in check. I'll probably need to make multiple blogs here, given the few interests I'm hoping to focus on. Or maybe just the one blog and multiple filters. I'll see how I want to approach that later. First, get started. Then optimize.

## Purging My Stuff

So I decided recently to take the radical step of paring things away. Getting rid of as much of what I have as I can. Be it digital or physical. Now I'm not looking to become an ascetic (though the idea is tempting). I'm simply trying to have my external environment be a bit more conducive to a focused approach to life. Because if I want to be able to see immediately the things that are important to me. And while I still will be incouragably curious, there's a certain point where you need to develop great depth of skill.

So I have started getting rid of extra things. Little bits and bobs. It's more of a starting place than anything. Getting rid of extras of this and that. Getting rid of low hanging fruit. I definitely feel like I'm easily overwhelmed. And I need to stop feeling bad about that.

I also need to stop feeling bad about needing to behave a different way, and make different choices. I have, historically, tried very hard to conform to other people's happy expectations of me and for me. Not always; I get overwhelmed and rebel as often as not. But certainly as life has gone, say, smoother in the past few years, conformity in ways has become the norm.

So I'm making this my declaration to myself, that I'm going to start being more me. I'm going to get rid of some of the things that I thought defined me, and focus on the things that *do* define me. We'll see where that leads.
